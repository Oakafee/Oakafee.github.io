Interpretation of New Jersey

Welcome. Here you will find interpretive information on various New Jersey related topics.

The goal of this website is to contribute to our understanding, appreciation, and love of the Garden State. I welcome submissions of all kinds about features of and experiences in the state.

The Land

Physiographic, geological, and ecological commentary on the state's natural aspect, with emphasis on the influence natural features have had on NJ's economy, history, and culture. All for the sake of the appreciation and understanding of this much-used, much-abused piece of earth.

We divide NJ into the Appalachian Ridge and Valley provence, the Highlands, the Pidemont (including the Watchung Mountains and Palisades), and the Coastal Plain. See a map of all of them here.

The People

Sketches of the society, politics, and culture of this enigmatic land. I hope to be able to say that this section contains a great diversity of perspectivese, so please contribute!

The Crazy Things People Have Done to the Land

Land use, infrastructure, development and its aftermath. This would include cities and towns, as well as other political divisions of land. We divide the state into its urban core, extensive suburbs, and outlying areas beyond the metropolis.


If you have any sense of NJ at all, whether as a resident, a visitor, or even just someone who has heard vague rumors about any part or aspect of the state, I would like to hear from you. Please contribute here.
