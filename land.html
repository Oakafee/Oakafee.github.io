The New Jersey Geological and Water Survey divides the state's remarkably diverse geography into four physiographic provinces:

Coastal Plain

Home to NJ's Atlantic Coast, Bayshores, and Pine Barrens

Piedmont

A fertile basin trasnected by igneous ridges, forming historically important natural barriers

Highlands

A rocky, mineral-rich hill country dotted with lakes and mountain streams

Appalachian Valley and Ridge

A very Pennsylvania-esque section of the Appalachian Mountains, dominated by Kitatinny Ridge
